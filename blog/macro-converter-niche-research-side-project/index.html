Why Do They All Strip Macros?

I pulled the documentation and support threads for the top 8 converters. The pattern is consistent:

  • CloudConvert: Strips macros — documented in their format conversion notes, liability reasoning cited
  • ILovePDF: Strips macros — confirmed in support responses
  • Zamzar: Strips macros — "for security, macros are not preserved"
  • SmallPDF: Strips macros — conversion engine doesn't support VBA preservation
  • Adobe Acrobat: Partial — strips macros in web conversion, preserves in desktop

These are all DA 70+ sites. Their business model requires them to strip macros. A platform serving 10 million conversions/month cannot risk forwarding one malicious VBA script.

The Competitive Gap

High DA competitors can't serve this gap without breaking their own security model. A platform that strips macros by policy cannot rank for "keep macros" without confusing their existing users. That's a structural moat for a new entrant.
